# Shot 01 — Walk to Station

**Duration:** 8 seconds
**Anchors:** F1 (Nervous Entry) → F2 (Starting Line)
**Apron state:** Pristine throughout
**Clock:** 60:00 (held — competition has not yet started)
**Emotional beat:** Nerves transitioning into focused readiness

---

## Frame anchors

**FIRST FRAME (F1):** Wide low-angle shot. Man [A1] in pristine white chef's apron, dark grey crew-neck tee with sleeves rolled, walks forward through the entrance of a vast competition kitchen [A2]. Black canvas knife roll over his right shoulder. Nineteen other contestants in matching white aprons walk in alongside him in soft focus. Industrial truss ceiling. Massive central digital countdown clock at the far end displaying **60:00** in bold orange-red.

**LAST FRAME (F2):** Tight medium shot. Same man at his stainless-steel cooking station. Both hands gripping the front edge of the counter, leaning forward slightly, looking up and across the kitchen at the central digital clock displaying **60:00**. Knife roll now unrolled flat in front of him with six chef knives laid out. Other contestants visible at their own stations in identical ready-pose, slightly out of focus.

---

## Universal style block

Photorealistic, broadcast-television cooking-competition aesthetic. Sony Venice cinema camera, 35mm prime lens, 16:9. Bright multi-source production lighting (overhead truss + kino-flo fill, ~4800K, no deep shadows). Skin tones natural and clearly readable. High dynamic range, vibrant saturation, sharp focus.

---

## Section 1 — Shot-by-shot effects timeline

```
SHOT 1.1 (00:00–00:01.5) — Group Walk-In
• EFFECT: Slow tracking dolly forward + speed ramp (deceleration)
• Wide low-angle hero composition, camera slowly tracking backward as the group walks toward camera
• 20 contestants in matching white aprons walking forward in soft sync
• Speed begins at ~85% (subtly slowed) and decelerates to ~70% over the beat
• Identifies the protagonist via slight focus pull toward him
• Subtle defined lens flare from overhead truss fixtures

SHOT 1.2 (00:01.5–00:03) — Peel-Off
• EFFECT: Focus pull (rack focus) + lateral tracking
• Camera stops backward dolly, holds, then begins lateral track to the right
• The protagonist breaks rhythm with the group, peels off toward his station, the others continue forward
• Rack focus from background contestants to him, sharpening on his face
• Speed normalizes to 100%
• His eyes scan ahead, finding his station

SHOT 1.3 (00:03–00:04.5) — Station Arrival
• EFFECT: Match-cut to medium shot + handheld settle
• Cut to a medium 3/4 shot of him reaching his station
• Camera handheld, micro-jittery, organic
• He sets the black canvas knife roll on the stainless-steel counter
• Station is bare except for the knife roll — clean, ready
• Background still soft from depth of field

SHOT 1.4 (00:04.5–00:06) — Knife Roll Unroll
• EFFECT: Top-down close-up + shallow depth of field
• Cut to overhead close-up of his hands on the knife roll, polished steel surface beneath
• His hands unfurl the canvas roll in one practiced motion — six chef knives revealed in order, gleaming
• Speed at ~80% — gives the unfurling weight without stopping the flow
• Specular highlight crawls along the blade edges as the roll opens

SHOT 1.5 (00:06–00:07.5) — Hands on Counter
• EFFECT: Cut to medium-tight + slight digital push-in
• Tight medium shot, eye-level, behind-counter framing
• His hands move from the knife roll to grip the front edge of the counter
• Subtle digital push-in (~5% scale) creates an unconscious lean toward him
• Background contestants now visible in soft focus, all settling at their own stations

SHOT 1.6 (00:07.5–00:08) — Look Up
• EFFECT: Low rise tilt-up + held breath
• Camera rises an inch and tilts up to follow his eye-line as he looks toward the giant digital clock above the pass
• Brief flash of the clock at **60:00** in bold orange-red, framed against the truss ceiling
• Cut back to his face — focused, locked, breath caught
• Hold the final beat — this IS the last frame (F2)
```

---

## Section 2 — Master effects inventory

1. **SLOW TRACKING DOLLY (used 1x)** — Shot 1.1. Establishing camera move; sets the heroic, deliberate tone.
2. **SPEED RAMP — DECELERATION (used 1x)** — Shot 1.1. Subtle ~85% → ~70% to add weight to the entrance.
3. **FOCUS PULL / RACK FOCUS (used 1x)** — Shot 1.2. Identifies the protagonist within the group.
4. **LATERAL TRACKING (used 1x)** — Shot 1.2. Smooth horizontal camera slide as he peels off.
5. **HANDHELD MICRO-JITTER (used 1x)** — Shot 1.3. Adds documentary realism to the station-arrival beat.
6. **TOP-DOWN OVERHEAD CLOSE-UP (used 1x)** — Shot 1.4. The signature framing for the knife-roll reveal.
7. **DIGITAL PUSH-IN (used 1x)** — Shot 1.5. ~5% subconscious scale to draw the eye in.
8. **TILT-UP + EYE-LINE FOLLOW (used 1x)** — Shot 1.6. Connects subject to the clock as the shot lands.
9. **SPECULAR HIGHLIGHT CRAWL (used 1x)** — Shot 1.4. Light moving along blade edges; tactile detail.

---

## Section 3 — Effects density map

```
00:00–00:03  =  MEDIUM DENSITY (slow dolly + ramp + rack focus + lateral track — 4 effects in 3s)
00:03–00:06  =  MEDIUM DENSITY (handheld settle + top-down + shallow DoF + specular crawl — 4 effects in 3s)
00:06–00:08  =  LOW DENSITY (digital push-in + tilt-up — 2 effects in 2s)
```

The shot intentionally de-escalates. The viewer arrives with the group, finds the protagonist, and ends in a quiet, held moment of breath. The clock will explode the silence in Shot 2.

---

## Section 4 — Energy arc

- **Beat 1 (0–3s) — Arrival:** Communal energy. He is one of many, but the camera marks him out. Adrenaline simmering, not boiling.
- **Beat 2 (3–6s) — Settling:** Solo focus. The knife roll unfurling is the first intimate craft moment. The viewer is invited in close.
- **Beat 3 (6–8s) — Held breath:** Stillness. Eyes up. Clock at 60:00. The film inhales.

The energy ENDS with the camera and subject both still. This makes the explosion of Shot 2 land harder.

---

## Sound design notes

- 0–3s: Ambient kitchen hum. Distant clatter. Soft footfalls of 20 contestants.
- 3–6s: A single low orchestral pulse begins. Knife-roll unfurl has its own subtle Foley — canvas, steel.
- 6–8s: Pulse holds. Held breath. We can hear his breathing. *Silence builds.*

## Negative prompt

dark moody lighting, deep shadows, dim kitchen, soft focus on the man, motion blur on the man's face, illegible clock, contestants in colors other than white aprons, deformed hands, extra fingers, painterly stylization, cartoon, anime, vignetting, faded colors, multiple men in the foreground (only ONE protagonist), competing background music in the prompt (sound design is separate).

---

## Director's notes

- The camera language is **deliberate and slow** here. This is the only shot in the film without urgency. Don't let it move fast.
- If Seedance drifts into more frenetic energy, regenerate with explicit "calm, deliberate, slow camera, slow movement, subdued energy."
- Lock the lighting and color from F1 — Shot 2 will inherit it. Any drift in skin tone or apron whiteness will betray the cut.
- The knife-roll unfurl is the small "wow" detail — give it the time and framing it deserves. If it feels rushed, push more of the 8-second budget toward Shot 1.4.
