# Shot 02 — Sprint to Pantry

**Duration:** 12 seconds
**Anchors:** F2 (Starting Line) → F3 (Pantry Storm)
**Apron state:** Pristine at start → first olive-oil streak across chest by end
**Clock:** 60:00 → 59:59 → falling fast (visible in the cuts)
**Emotional beat:** Pure adrenaline / chaos / hunting

---

## Frame anchors

**FIRST FRAME (F2):** Tight medium shot. Man at his station, hands gripping counter edge, looking up at clock displaying **60:00**. Pristine apron. Knife roll unrolled in front of him. Other contestants at their stations in matching ready-pose.

**LAST FRAME (F3):** Medium dynamic shot. Man in the pantry [A3], arms full of fresh basil, rosemary, three lemons, butcher-paper-wrapped meat in left arm; olive oil bottle and capers jar in right hand. Mid-stride twisting to shoulder past another contestant who is reaching for a shelf. Apron now bears a single fresh glossy olive-oil streak across the chest. Pantry visibly chaotic with multiple contestants in motion behind.

---

## Universal style block

Photorealistic, broadcast-television cooking-competition aesthetic. Sony Venice cinema camera, 35mm prime lens, 16:9. Bright multi-source production lighting (overhead truss + kino-flo fill, ~4800K, no deep shadows). Skin tones natural. Vibrant saturation. Sharp focus.

---

## Section 1 — Shot-by-shot effects timeline

```
SHOT 2.1 (00:00–00:01) — Clock Flip
• EFFECT: Extreme close-up + speed ramp (acceleration into the flip)
• ECU on the giant digital clock face — the orange-red digits filling the frame
• "60:00" holds for half a beat, then digits flip aggressively to "59:59"
• Subtle film-grain crawl on the LED segments
• Shallow depth of field, the rest of the kitchen melts into orange-red bokeh
• Speed ramps from 50% (frozen 60:00) to 120% (the flip moment)
• Hard buzzer SFX cue accompanies the flip in sound design

SHOT 2.2 (00:01–00:02) — Wide Kitchen Explosion
• EFFECT: Whip-cut from ECU + wide overhead reveal + speed ramp (acceleration)
• Whip-cut transition: blur smear connects the clock close-up to the wide shot
• Top-down high-angle wide of the entire kitchen — all 20 stations and their contestants
• Every contestant simultaneously erupts from their station and breaks into a sprint toward the back of the kitchen
• Speed at ~115% — slightly faster than real, hyperreal urgency
• Camera holds static; the chaos plays out within the frame

SHOT 2.3 (00:02–00:04) — Tracking Sprint
• EFFECT: Steadicam profile-track + selective focus + heavy directional motion blur on background
• Camera tracks the protagonist in profile as he sprints across the kitchen floor
• Camera matches his pace, holding him sharp in the center while the kitchen behind him streaks with motion blur
• His feet pound the polished black floor — micro-flares of reflected truss light at each footfall
• Other contestants visible as motion-blurred forms running parallel to him
• His knife roll left behind on the station — clean cinematic detail

SHOT 2.4 (00:04–00:05) — Pantry Doors Burst
• EFFECT: Low-angle hero shot + push-in dolly + slight slow-motion (80%)
• Cut to low-angle on the pantry doors as he reaches them, three other contestants flanking
• He shoves the doors open with both hands at speed, doors slamming outward
• Brief slow-motion (~80%) at the moment of contact captures the swing of the doors
• Pantry interior bursts into view as the doors open — overflowing shelves, warmer lighting

SHOT 2.5 (00:05–00:07) — Pantry Chaos Reveal
• EFFECT: Cinematic crane move (descending) + multi-subject coverage
• Crane move: starts at ceiling height looking down at the chaos, then descends to chest height
• Reveals the full pantry interior — towering shelves on both sides, herbs hanging from above, multiple contestants grabbing ingredients in chaotic motion
• The descent introduces him as one figure among many, then isolates him as he emerges into the foreground
• Warm pendant amber light from above mixes with cool industrial overhead — rich color contrast

SHOT 2.6 (00:07–00:09) — Hands Grabbing Ingredients
• EFFECT: Rapid-cut macro montage — 4 cuts in 2s
• 00:07.0–00:07.5 — His left hand grabbing a bunch of fresh basil from a hanging cluster, leaves slightly trembling
• 00:07.5–00:08.0 — Right hand seizing a glass olive oil bottle by the neck
• 00:08.0–00:08.5 — Left hand cradling three lemons, plucked one-after-another
• 00:08.5–00:09.0 — Right hand snatching a butcher-paper-wrapped chunk of meat from the chilled center display
• Each macro cut sharp focus, shallow depth of field, frame filled with texture

SHOT 2.7 (00:09–00:11) — The Shoulder-Past
• EFFECT: Slow-motion (~30% speed) + camera shake + bokeh background
• Pull back from macro to medium 3/4 shot
• He twists his torso, leading shoulder forward, to push past another contestant who is reaching for the same shelf
• Approximately 30% speed — the moment of contact stretches
• Subtle camera shake on the impact of the shoulder
• Background contestants out of focus, but visibly chaotic
• His arms loaded with the ingredients gathered in 2.6
• During this beat, a smear of olive oil from the bottle in his right hand visibly marks his apron — the first stain of the film

SHOT 2.8 (00:11–00:12) — Lock to F3
• EFFECT: Speed normalization (~30% → 100%) + held final beat
• Speed restores to normal as he completes the shoulder-past
• Last frame: he is mid-stride twisted past the obstacle, arms full, apron streaked, eyes laser-locked on his next target shelf out of frame
• This is F3 — hold for the last quarter-second
```

---

## Section 4 — Energy arc

- **Beat 1 (0–2s) — Detonation:** Clock flip + kitchen explosion. Adrenaline maxes out instantly.
- **Beat 2 (2–7s) — Velocity:** Sprint, doors burst, crane reveals the pantry. Sustained chaos.
- **Beat 3 (7–11s) — Hunting:** Macro montage of grabbing + the slowed shoulder-past. The viewer feels his hands.
- **Beat 4 (11–12s) — Lock:** Speed normalizes. We arrive at F3, breath held, ingredients secured.

The shot is a single inhale-and-explode. It does not let up until the final half-second.

---

## Sound design notes

- 0–1s: Held silence from Shot 1, then a sudden buzzer cue on the clock flip.
- 1–2s: Full kitchen war — twenty pairs of footfalls, shouts, scraping chairs, pans clanging.
- 2–7s: Aggressive percussive score under continuing kitchen Foley.
- 7–9s: The macro montage carries its own Foley — basil rustle, glass-against-shelf, paper rustle.
- 9–11s: As the slow-motion hits, audio drops slightly into a low rumble. Heartbeat under the mix.
- 11–12s: Audio surges back as speed normalizes. Lands on F3 with a final percussive accent.

---

## Negative prompt

slow pacing, calm energy, single contestant alone in pantry, empty pantry, dim or moody lighting, deep shadows, illegible clock numbers, blurry protagonist face, motion blur on the man's face, contestants politely walking (they must be sprinting), painterly stylization, cartoon, anime, deformed hands or fingers, falling ingredients (he holds them firmly), apron pristine in the last frame (it MUST have the olive-oil streak by F3).

## Director's notes

- This shot has more cuts (8 sub-shots) and effects (15+) than any other in the first half. If Seedance struggles with the volume, prioritize: Shot 2.1 (clock flip), Shot 2.5 (crane reveal), and Shot 2.7 (shoulder-past slow-mo). Those three carry the energy.
- The olive-oil streak appearing in 2.7 is a critical continuity moment. If Seedance doesn't add it, you may need to generate this shot in two halves and composite the streak with a stain plate.
- Compared to Shot 1's deliberate calm, this shot should feel like a different *speed* of camera — handheld, urgent, faster cuts. Same lighting, different rhythm.
- The crane descent in 2.5 is the most ambitious move in the shot. If it's not landing, swap for a static wide of the pantry interior with all the chaos playing out in frame — same emotional impact, simpler execution.
