## Image prompt

> Photorealistic still, 16:9, shot on a Sony Venice cinema camera with a 35mm prime lens. Television cooking competition production aesthetic.
>
> **Composition:** Tight medium shot, eye-level. The man [A1] stands behind his stainless-steel cooking station in the foreground, both hands gripping the front edge of the counter, leaning forward very slightly, looking up and across the kitchen toward the giant central digital countdown clock. Camera is angled to capture both him in the foreground and the clock display in the background, depth-of-field connecting them.
>
> **Subject:** The man [A1] in his pristine plain white chef's apron tied at the waist, dark grey crew-neck cotton t-shirt with sleeves rolled to mid-forearm, white kitchen towel folded crisply tucked into the apron strings on his right hip. Both hands grip the front edge of the stainless steel counter — knuckles slightly tense, not white. His shoulders are squared, chest open. His expression is intensely focused, jaw locked, lips pressed thin. Eyes are wide and fixed on the clock. He is breathing.
>
> **Foreground / station detail:** On the stainless steel station in front of him, his black canvas knife roll is now unrolled flat, revealing six gleaming chef's knives of varying sizes laid out in order. A small rectangular cutting board, a clean dish towel, and a single empty stainless-steel bowl. Otherwise the station is bare and ready.
>
> **Background:** Behind him, the full kitchen [A2] stretches into depth. Slightly out of focus but clearly readable: nineteen other contestants at their own stations, each in identical pose — leaning, ready, looking up at the clock. The other stations are mirror images of his. Tall stainless cookware racks line the side walls. Industrial truss ceiling 25 feet up with bare-tube fixtures and softboxes blazing. The massive central digital countdown clock hangs above the pass at the far end, displaying **60:00** in bold orange-red digits on a black face — sharp, clearly readable.
>
> **Lighting:** Bright multi-source production lighting. Hard key from overhead truss with soft kino-flo fill from both sides. Color temperature ~4800K. No deep shadows. The orange-red of the clock pops against the cool steel and dark floor.
>
> **Color & focus:** High dynamic range. Vibrant saturation. Sharp focus on the man and his station, slight DoF falloff to the contestants behind, but the clock at the far end is rendered crisp and legible (deep DoF, classic broadcast technique).

## Negative prompt

motion blur, soft focus on subject, illegible clock, contestants in colors other than white aprons, deep shadows, vignetting, painterly stylization, deformed face or hands, cluttered station with extra props.

## Generation notes

- **Continuity check vs F1:** same lighting, same skin tone, same apron crispness, same hair. If skin tone shifts even slightly between F1 and F2, the cut will betray it. Lock the lighting and color from F1 first.
- The clock reads the same as F1 (60:00) — this is intentional. The video transition from F1→F2 covers the walk-in and the settling at the station; time hasn't started yet.
- The other contestants in identical pose is a critical visual rhyme. Don't let them break formation — explicitly prompt "all contestants in matching pose, all looking at clock."
- If the clock comes out illegible, re-emphasize "digital countdown clock '60:00' sharply rendered, large bold orange-red digits clearly visible."
