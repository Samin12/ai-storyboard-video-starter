# Assets Needed

Two categories: **photo plates of yourself** (you film these), and **Omni Reference assets** (you generate these in Nano Banana from the plates plus prompts).

Lock everything below before generating any frame.

---

## Part 1 — Photo plates of yourself (P1–P7)

**Wardrobe:** plain white chef's apron over a dark grey crew-neck cotton t-shirt with sleeves rolled to mid-forearm. Plain dark slim-fit chinos or jeans. White cotton kitchen towel tucked into the apron strings on the right hip. Black canvas knife roll bag for P1.

**Setup:** plain neutral wall. Soft window light from one side, fill from a white reflector or wall on the other. Wear the apron through the entire shoot — let it get dirty naturally as the day goes on. **Don't fake the stains** — actually cook something or smear the apron deliberately as you go.

**Apron progression during the shoot:**
- Start clean (P1, P2)
- Add a single olive-oil streak before P3
- Add flour smudges + herb-oil smear + soot before P4 and P5 (same state)
- Trash it with flour, sauce splatter (use ketchup or roasted-pepper purée for the red), and a torch-singe before P6 and P7

| Plate | Pose | Used in frames | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1 — Master portrait** | Front-facing, neutral expression, even soft light, plain background. Both shoulders square to camera. Eyes directly at lens. Apron pristine. | All frames (used to build A1) | This is the single most important shot. Take 20+ takes; pick the best. |
| **P2 — Walking forward** | 3/4 angle, mid-stride, right foot forward, knife roll over right shoulder held by right hand, jaw set, eyes forward and slightly up. Apron pristine. | Frame 01 | Need motion in the legs but a still face. |
| **P3 — Hands gripping counter** | Both hands gripping the front edge of a counter or table at hip height, leaning forward slightly, eyes looking up and to the side as if at a clock. Apron pristine. | Frame 02 | Capture the held breath. |
| **P4 — Loaded arms, sprinting/twisting** | Both arms full of fake ingredients (or props), torso twisted as if shouldering past, mid-stride leaning forward, eyes laser-focused off-camera. Apron with first stains. | Frame 03 | Most action-heavy plate. |
| **P5 — Hands hovering over plated dish** | Standing at a counter, both hands pulled back from the plate with fingers spread, leaning down slightly to examine, small private smile playing at the corners of the mouth. Apron with working stains. | Frame 04 | The smile is critical — small, private, quiet pride. |
| **P6 — Frozen in shock** | Standing still, both hands raised to chest height palms facing each other (as if holding something invisible), mouth open but not screaming, eyes wide and unfocused looking down. Apron same as P5. | Frame 05 | Same outfit-state as P5. The shock must be frozen, not theatrical. |
| **P7 — Plating the comeback** | Standing at a counter, hands pulling back from a plate, fingers slightly extended, exhausted face, breath visible. Apron WRECKED. | Frame 06 | Apron must be visibly destroyed by this point. |
| **P8 — Disbelief / near-laugh** | Standing tall, both arms slightly out from body palms forward, mouth open in near-laugh, eyes wet. Apron WRECKED. | Frame 07 | Hardest to act. Watch a video of someone genuinely surprised on a competition show for reference. |

**Optional bonus plates (recommended):**
- **P9 — Hands close-up:** Tight on hands chopping or working a knife. Useful as a cutaway in any video segment.
- **P10 — Wide back-of-head shot:** Standing at a station, back to camera, looking forward at the kitchen. Useful for any establishing shot.

## Build order

1. **P1–P8** (one afternoon shoot, in order, with apron progression)
2. **A1** (face master) — generate from P1
3. **A2** (kitchen) — establishes the world
4. **A4, A5, A6** (the three dishes) — these are the visual spine of the emotional arc, get them perfect as standalone stills
5. **A7, A8, A9** (the three judges) — generate as character portraits
6. **A3, A10** (pantry, pass) — supporting environments
7. **Frames 01–07** in order, using the assets

---

## Minimum viable variant

If you want to test the pipeline before committing to the full asset library:

- Shoot **only P1** of yourself.
- Build **only A1** (face), **A2** (kitchen), and **A4** (peak dish).
- Generate **only F2** (Starting Line) and **F4** (The Peak).
- Generate **one video** between them.

That gets you a 10-second proof-of-concept clip in a fraction of the time. If it works, build the full thing. If it doesn't, you've burned a day instead of a week.
