# Seedance 2.0 Video Prompts — Frames 1–6

**Model:** `seedance_2_0` (Bytedance, via Higgsfield)
**Aspect ratio:** `9:16` vertical
**Duration per clip:** 5 seconds
**Resolution:** `1080p`
**Mode:** `std`
**Genre:** `auto`
**Reference per clip:** the corresponding generated frame as `start_image` (locks identity + framing).

Each clip animates the static frame into ~5 seconds of motion that supports the dialogue line from `03-shot-list/approved/shot-list.md`. No camera moves that change product identity or hide the label.

---

## Clip 1 — HOOK (animates Frame 1)
**Prompt:**
> A young woman holds an orange-labeled vitamin bottle close to a phone selfie camera. She gestures with the bottle toward the lens with an animated, surprised expression — eyes widening, slight head shake, mouth moving naturally as if speaking. The bottle stays in clear view, label squared to camera, never tilting away. Subtle handheld micro-shake. Soft morning daylight, kitchen blur behind her. Authentic UGC iPhone selfie feel.

**Lip-sync target dialogue:** "Wait — I was taking FIVE different vitamins every morning... until I found this one bottle."

---

## Clip 2 — PROBLEM (animates Frame 2)
**Prompt:**
> Top-down overhead shot of a kitchen counter covered in many different generic vitamin bottles. A woman's hand sweeps across the bottles in mock frustration, knocking one or two slightly. A capsule rolls. The camera holds steady, slight handheld float. Warm morning light, no people's faces visible — just the cluttered counter and the sweeping hand.

**Lip-sync target dialogue (voice-over, no on-screen mouth):** "Vitamin D, K2, magnesium, zinc, B-complex — my counter looked like a pharmacy. And I never even remembered which one to take."

---

## Clip 3 — REVEAL (animates Frame 3)
**Prompt:**
> Medium eye-level shot. A woman in a cream sweater holds an orange-labeled vitamin bottle at chest height, label squared and 100% readable. She looks at the camera, smiles confidently, gives a small, proud nod, and tilts the bottle very slightly to catch the light — but the label stays facing the camera the whole time. Mouth moves naturally as she speaks. Soft window light from the left. Tripod-steady, no zoom.

**Lip-sync target dialogue:** "Then I found Dr. Berg's D3 and K2. Ten thousand IU of D, plus K2 — both in one capsule."

---

## Clip 4 — DEMONSTRATION (animates Frame 4)
**Prompt:**
> Close-up of a woman's hand tipping a single white oval capsule from an orange-labeled vitamin bottle into her open palm. The capsule lands with a tiny bounce. She picks up a glass of water with the other hand. Sharp focus on the capsule and palm; the bottle stays in soft focus in the background with its orange label still recognizable. Subtle handheld feel. Warm morning kitchen light.

**Lip-sync target dialogue (voice-over):** "One capsule a day. That's it. Immunity, bones, mood, energy — all covered."

---

## Clip 5 — PROOF (animates Frame 5)
**Prompt:**
> Slow gentle dolly-in on an orange-labeled vitamin bottle sitting on a sunlit white windowsill. A morning sunbeam crosses the label, light catching the orange gradient. Subtle dust particles drift in the beam. The bottle is the only subject — no people. No rotation, no tilt — the label stays facing the camera throughout. Cinematic but still phone-shot.

**Lip-sync target dialogue (voice-over, no on-screen mouth):** "10,000 IU is the dose that actually does something. Made in the USA. 120 capsules — that's a four-month supply."

## Clip 6 — CTA (animates Frame 6)
**Prompt:**
> Selfie-style medium shot. The same young woman in a cream sweater holds an orange-labeled vitamin bottle next to her cheek, smiling broadly. She points at the label with her other hand and gives a friendly little nudge gesture toward the camera, mouth moving naturally as she speaks. The label stays facing the camera the whole time. Soft handheld float, morning kitchen window light. Authentic UGC selfie energy.

**Lip-sync target dialogue:** "Stop wasting money on five separate bottles. Link's in my bio — go grab one."
