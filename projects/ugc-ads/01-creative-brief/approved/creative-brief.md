# Creative Brief — Dr. Berg D3 & K2 UGC Ad

## One-sentence premise
A real-feeling woman in her kitchen explains why she ditched her shelf of separate vitamins for one bottle of Dr. Berg's D3 & K2 — and walks the viewer through the benefits that convinced her.

## Format
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical, TikTok / Reels / Shorts)
- **Total length:** ~30 seconds, 6 shots × ~5 seconds each
- **Style:** UGC-authentic. Phone-shot feel, natural light, no studio polish. Direct-to-camera dialogue.
- **Talent:** female creator, late 20s–early 30s, warm, conversational, "skeptical-friend-who-just-tried-it" energy.
- **Setting:** her own kitchen / breakfast nook, morning light, plants, neutral modern home.

## Audience
Health-curious adults 28–55 who already buy supplements, are tired of taking 5+ pills, and are open to "one-bottle-does-it-all" claims if they sound credible.

## Emotional arc
Surprise → relatable frustration → relief at discovery → confident endorsement → friendly nudge to buy.

## Structure (the 6 beats)
1. **Hook** — pattern interrupt + bottle close to camera.
2. **Problem** — counter cluttered with too many vitamin bottles.
3. **Reveal** — clean shot of the Dr. Berg bottle, label readable.
4. **Demonstration** — taking the capsule with water, casual.
5. **Proof** — bottle in hero light, USP voiced over.
6. **CTA** — direct line to viewer + "link in bio".

## Continuity locks
- Same creator throughout (same face, same outfit, same kitchen).
- Same bottle, same label orientation when product is on screen (per `02-references/approved/product-reference.md`).
- Warm morning daylight throughout (no harsh studio lighting, no nighttime).

## What must NEVER appear
- Medical disease claims ("cures", "treats", "prevents [disease]").
- Doctor characters in white coats giving prescriptions.
- Lab / clinical / pharmacy environments.
- Other competing supplement brand bottles (the cluttered shelf in shot 2 must be unbranded or generic-labeled bottles).
