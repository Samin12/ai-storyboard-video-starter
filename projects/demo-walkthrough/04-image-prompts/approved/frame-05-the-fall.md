**Narrative role:** End of Shot 4 / Start of Shot 5.
**Clock:** 08:42
**Apron state:** Identical to F4 — same olive-oil streak, flour smudges, herb oil smear, soot marks. **No new stains.** This frame is in the same outfit-state as F4 because the chaos hasn't physically touched him yet — only his dish.

The herb oil pooled in vivid green next to the largest porcelain shard. The single nasturtium flower lying upside-down a foot from the wreckage, a casualty.

> **Subject:** The man [A1] in the upper part of the frame, standing perfectly still. His apron is in the F4 state — olive-oil streak, flour smudges, herb oil smear, soot marks, towel yanked. **No new chaos on the apron.** Both his hands are raised to chest height, palms facing each other as if still holding the plate that's no longer there. His eyes are wide and unfocused, looking down at the wreckage but seeing through it. Hair messy. Sweat at his temples. He has not moved in seconds.

Other contestants in the soft middle and far ground are still cooking — pans flickering, knives moving, bodies in motion. They have not noticed. The massive central digital countdown clock above the pass is visible in the background displaying **08:42** in bold orange-red digits — falling. Industrial truss ceiling overhead. The contrast between his stillness and the kitchen's continued motion is the entire visual point.
>
> **Lighting:** Bright multi-source production lighting. Same hard key from overhead truss, same kino-flo fill from sides. Color temperature ~4800K. No deep shadows. The wreckage in the foreground catches a clean specular highlight from above — every porcelain shard and droplet of sauce is sharply lit. His face is fully readable.
>
> **Color & focus:** High dynamic range. The vivid green herb oil and the orange nasturtium flower in the wreckage are the most saturated elements in the frame — beautiful even in destruction. Sharp focus on the wreckage in the foreground, slightly softer focus on him in the upper third (DoF separation), but the clock at the back remains crisp and legible.

## Generation notes

- This is the technical hardest frame. The camera must be low enough to make the wreckage feel huge but high enough that we can find his face. Prompt for "low angle near floor, camera tilted upward, dish wreckage dominant in foreground, subject in upper third of frame."
- His face must look *frozen,* not emoting. Mouth open but not strained. If the face reads as "screaming" or "crying," regenerate with "face frozen in shock, blank stare, no emotional intensity, vacant expression."
- The continuity rule for the apron is critical: it MUST match F4 exactly. New stains here would imply the comeback has already started, which kills the emotional beat.
- Sound design at this cut goes to silence. Frame should look quiet despite kitchen motion behind.
- If A5 (the wreckage reference) hasn't been built yet, generate it as a standalone tabletop still first, then bring into this frame.
