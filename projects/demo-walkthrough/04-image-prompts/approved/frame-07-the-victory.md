## Generation notes

- This is the bookend to F1. Match the lighting, color grade, and skin tone of F1 exactly so the film visually closes its loop.
- The "near-laugh, wet-eyed disbelief" expression is hardest to land. Prompt for "face caught between laughing and crying, eyes wet but composed, mouth open in small surprised laugh, real not performed, the expression of someone who didn't believe they could win." If it reads as theatrical or grinning ear-to-ear, regenerate.
- The judges' faces are essential. Each is its own micro-portrait. Prompt explicitly: "the Veteran with hard-won small smile, the Critic with quiet warm smile, the Warm Heart grinning broadly — three different smiles, three different characters." If they all look the same, regenerate emphasizing distinct expressions.
- The picked-at dish on the pass is a critical detail viewers may not consciously notice but absolutely register. It tells them the judging happened.
- The slightly warmer column of light on him is the only stylistic flourish — keep it subtle. If it reads as cheesy "spotlight from heaven," dial it back.
- Hold a beat extra long on this frame in the final edit. It earned the time.
